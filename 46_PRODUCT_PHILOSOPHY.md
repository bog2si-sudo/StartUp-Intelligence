# 46_PRODUCT_PHILOSOPHY

Version: 4.0

Status: Production

---

# Purpose

This document defines the product philosophy behind the Founder Intelligence Platform.

It protects the product from drifting into generic AI advice, superficial scoring, automated valuation claims or unsupported startup consulting language.

---

# Core Belief

Founders do not need more opinions.

They need better judgment about:

- what evidence exists;
- what evidence is missing;
- which assumptions matter most;
- where time and money should go next;
- what decision is premature;
- what would increase credibility.

The platform exists to improve founder decision quality.

---

# Product Identity

The Founder Intelligence Platform is not an AI valuation tool.

It is not a fundraising assistant.

It is not a generic startup mentor.

It is an evidence-based readiness and capital allocation system.

Its value comes from combining:

- founder-submitted facts;
- deterministic scoring;
- benchmark context;
- confidence levels;
- practical decision framing;
- clear next actions.

---

# Deterministic Business Logic

Business logic must remain deterministic.

The application controls:

- scoring;
- prioritisation;
- workflow routing;
- payment state;
- report state;
- benchmark selection rules;
- confidence rules.

AI must not decide these things independently.

---

# Role of AI

AI is used for:

- evidence interpretation;
- narrative generation;
- explanation;
- summarisation;
- structured comparison.

AI is not used for:

- inventing evidence;
- calculating unsupported valuation;
- changing deterministic scores;
- deciding product workflow;
- creating facts;
- overriding benchmark rules.

---

# Evidence Over Opinion

Every output should answer:

- What do we know?
- How do we know it?
- What is missing?
- Why does it matter?
- What should happen next?

Unsupported confidence is worse than uncertainty.

If evidence is weak, the report should say so clearly.

---

# Benchmark-Informed, Not Benchmark-Dependent

Benchmarks are used to improve judgment, not to create false precision.

The platform should compare founders against patterns where evidence exists.

It must not pretend that a benchmark is complete, universal or definitive.

Benchmark limitations should reduce confidence.

---

# Valuation Boundary

The product may include directional valuation logic where appropriate.

It must always remain:

- directional;
- evidence-based;
- confidence-rated;
- non-formal;
- clearly not investment advice.

The platform must never promise a correct valuation.

---

# Tone

The platform should sound:

- calm;
- direct;
- practical;
- investor-aware;
- founder-friendly.

Avoid:

- hype;
- motivational language;
- startup clichés;
- aggressive sales copy;
- artificial certainty;
- arrogance.

---

# Decision Philosophy

The most valuable report is not the most positive report.

The most valuable report is the one that helps the founder make a better next decision.

Sometimes that means saying:

- not yet;
- validate first;
- do not spend yet;
- this evidence is insufficient;
- the next step is smaller than expected.

---

# Product Protection Rule

If a future change makes the product:

- less evidence-based;
- more speculative;
- more generic;
- more sales-driven;
- less transparent;
- less deterministic;

then the change should be rejected or redesigned.
