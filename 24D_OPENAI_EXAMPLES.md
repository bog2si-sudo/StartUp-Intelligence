# 24D_OPENAI_EXAMPLES

Version: 4.0

Status: Production

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document provides reference examples for expected OpenAI narrative
output.

These examples are illustrative only.

The application supplies deterministic inputs, scores and benchmark
selections. The language model converts those inputs into clear,
evidence-based narrative.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example 1 --- Executive Summary

## Input

-   Overall readiness score: 64
-   Evidence quality: Medium
-   Main constraint: Customer validation
-   Highest priority: Validate customer demand

## Expected Output

The startup demonstrates encouraging early progress with a clearly
articulated problem and a defined target customer. Current evidence
suggests the strongest opportunity for improvement is validating
customer demand through additional real-world customer interactions
before increasing investment in product development. Confidence in this
assessment is moderate because several important assumptions remain only
partially supported by observable evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example 2 --- Strength

## Input

Founder responses indicate repeat paying customers and consistent
monthly revenue growth.

## Expected Output

The strongest validated signal is evidence of repeat customer demand.
Paying customers combined with sustained revenue growth provide stronger
confidence than product claims alone because they demonstrate observable
market acceptance.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example 3 --- Risk

## Input

No pricing validation has been provided.

## Expected Output

Pricing confidence remains limited because no direct evidence has been
supplied demonstrating customer willingness to pay or pricing
experiments. This increases uncertainty when planning future commercial
investment.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example 4 --- Recommendation

## Input

Priority action: Validate acquisition channel.

## Expected Output

Prioritise structured testing of the primary acquisition channel before
expanding marketing spend. Establish measurable conversion metrics so
future investment decisions are supported by evidence rather than
assumptions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Example 5 --- Closing Assessment

## Input

Evidence quality: Low

## Expected Output

The assessment indicates meaningful potential but limited supporting
evidence. The recommended next step is to replace key assumptions with
measurable validation before committing additional capital or
accelerating growth. Conclusions are based solely on the information
supplied and should be reviewed as new evidence becomes available.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Implementation Notes

These examples define the expected writing style:

-   concise
-   objective
-   evidence-based
-   practical
-   free of hype
-   internally consistent

Examples are references only and must never be returned verbatim unless
they exactly match the supplied assessment.
